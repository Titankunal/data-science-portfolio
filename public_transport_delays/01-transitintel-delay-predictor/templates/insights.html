Understanding XGBoost

Our predictor runs on eXtreme Gradient Boosting (XGBoost), a robust machine learning algorithm that builds hundreds of decision trees. Each subsequent tree learns from the mistakes of its predecessors. This ensemble approach allows us to find complex, hidden non-linear relationships in weather, traffic, and events to produce a highly accurate delay probability.

How to Read This: Out of 400 test trips, the model perfectly identified almost all actual delays (293 out of 300). However, the high false positive rate (97) means it tends to be highly cautious and over-predict delays.

1. Class Imbalance

Our historical dataset naturally contains ~75% delayed trips. Despite weighting training algorithms heavily, the model adopts a conservative stance, favoring delay predictions to maximize safety.

2. Extreme Recall

The XGBoost engine achieves a phenomenal 98% Recall on delayed trips. If a trip is genuinely going to be delayed, the model has a 98% chance of warning you in advance.

3. Complex Routes Rule

Internal diagnostic mapping indicates that specific Route IDs overshadow weather or traffic. Structural route design directly contributes the most to likelihood of a delay occurring.
